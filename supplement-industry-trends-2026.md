# Supplement Industry Trends 2026 — GLP-1, Hydration & Recovery
### Industry Intelligence Report for Supplement Operators

---

## Executive Summary

The supplement industry in 2026 is undergoing a structural shift from commodity wellness products to targeted, outcome-specific formulations. Three categories are leading this transformation: GLP-1 support supplements, functional hydration products, and recovery formulas. The VMS (Vitamins, Minerals & Supplements) market has evolved from a multivitamin business into a clinically-backed, consumer-driven category. GLP-1 medications — now used by 10+ million Americans — are the single biggest demand catalyst since protein powder, creating a $1.2B hydration market projected to reach $5.8B by 2036.

## Market Macro Context

- 75% of Americans now use dietary supplements, with 91% considering them vital to their health

- Key structural shift in 2026: Brands framing products around *complementary needs* to GLP-1 drugs (muscle maintenance, nutrient sufficiency, metabolic support, hydration) rather than competing with prescription therapies or making unsubstantiated weight loss claims

## GLP-1 Support Supplements: The Category Defining 2026

GLP-1 medications (semaglutide/Ozempic, tirzepatide/Mounjaro) are reshaping the supplement market. J.P. Morgan projects ~30 million Americans on GLP-1 therapy by 2030. Global incretin treatment market forecast to reach $200B.

### GLP-1 User Nutritional Gap Categories
- **Protein insufficiency**: Reduced appetite = inadequate protein intake = muscle loss risk
- **Electrolyte depletion**: GLP-1 reduces food and fluid intake → dehydration, low sodium/potassium/magnesium
- **Micronutrient deficiency**: B12, vitamin D, calcium, and iron commonly flagged in GLP-1 users
- **Gut sensitivity**: Nausea, constipation, and digestive disruption requiring gut-supportive formulations
- **Muscle preservation**: Users prioritizing lean-mass retention need creatine and HMB support

## Hydration Products

GLP-1 hydration market: $1.2B in 2026, projected $5.8B by 2036 at 17% CAGR. Electrolyte replenishment leads at 33% of market.

### Competitive Landscape
- **LMNT**: 1,000mg Na, 200mg K, 60mg Mg, 0g sugar — Athletic/keto. Extreme sodium not ideal for GLP-1 digestive sensitivity.
- **Liquid I.V.**: 500mg Na, 370mg K, ~11g sugar — 11g sugar problematic for glucose-conscious GLP-1 users.
- **Cure**: Low-sugar, stevia/monk fruit — Good GLP-1 fit. Underutilizing GLP-1 positioning.
- **Pedialyte**: Medical-grade credibility. Not positioned as lifestyle product.

**White space**: Hydration product with GLP-1-specific positioning, clean ingredients, gut-supportive formulation, low sugar, speaks directly to GLP-1 + active user.

## Claims Under Scrutiny — GLP-1 Adjacent

### FTC/FDA 2026 Enforcement Focus

**FTC "result claims" crackdown**: Testimonial-driven weight loss claims under heightened scrutiny in GLP-1 support category where the drug is often doing the work.

**GLP-1 adjacent positioning**: Language like "as powerful as GLP-1" or "the natural Ozempic" faces significant regulatory and legal risk. FDA monitors structure/function claims aggressively in this space.

**"Clinically proven" language**: Brands must distinguish between ingredients that are clinically studied (e.g., creatine) and products that are "clinically proven."

**Third-party certification**: NSF International, Informed Sport, and USP certifications increasingly important as protection against quality-related scrutiny.

### Safe vs. Unsafe GLP-1 Adjacent Claim Language

**SAFE (compliant):**
- ✅ "Supports hydration and electrolyte balance"
- ✅ "Formulated to complement a GLP-1 lifestyle"
- ✅ "Supports muscle maintenance during calorie restriction"
- ✅ "Supports healthy metabolism"
- ✅ "Supports appetite management"
- ✅ "Supports healthy blood sugar levels already in normal range"
- ✅ "Supports weight management as part of a healthy diet"

**UNSAFE (non-compliant):**
- ❌ "Replaces what GLP-1 depletes" — implies drug causation
- ❌ "Works like Ozempic" / "Works like semaglutide" — drug equivalence claim
- ❌ "The natural GLP-1" / "Natural Ozempic" / "Natural semaglutide" — branded drug comparison
- ❌ "Boosts GLP-1 levels" / "Increases GLP-1 secretion" — drug pathway claim
- ❌ "GLP-1 receptor agonist" — explicit drug mechanism claim
- ❌ "As powerful as GLP-1" / "As effective as Ozempic" — drug equivalence
